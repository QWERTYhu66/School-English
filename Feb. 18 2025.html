Feb. 18, 2025 - The 57 Bus

Character Q&A: Sasha Fleischman (Pronouns: They/Them)

What are their interests/hobbies?

Sasha finds their interests in linguistics, fantasy fiction, role-playing card games, gender studies, LGBTQ+ advocacy, Russia, and Communism.

What changes this character?

Sasha's life is changed by the 57 bus attack, which brings trauma but strengthens their resilience and advocacy.

What is their ethnicity?

Sasha is Caucasian-American.

What problems did they face?

Throughout the book, Sasha experiences gender identity struggles, societal misunderstanding, physical and emotional trauma from the attack.

How do their characteristics reflect on the setting?

Their characteristics highlights Oakland's diversity and tensions between progressive and traditional views.

How are they a role model?

Sasha is a role model by showing resilience, self-acceptance, empathy, and forgiveness throughout the entirety of the book.

What is their backstory?

Sasha grew up in a supportive, middle-class Oakland family, attending a progressive school.

What is their relationship with the secondary characters?

Sasha have supportive family and friends; forgives Richard despite the attack.

What is their personality?

Sasha is intelligent, introspective, confident, and compassionate.

Character Q&A: Richard Thomas

What are their interests/hobbies?

Richard is interested in basketball, joking with friends, trying to stay out of trouble.

What changes this character?

Setting Sasha's skirt on fire, leading to deep regret, legal consequences, and personal growth.

What is their ethnicity?

Richard is African-American.

What problems did they face?

Richard faces numerous problems, however, the main problems that are present in his life are poverty, systemic racism, an unstable home life, and harsh legal penalties.

How do their characteristics reflect on the setting?

Richard's characteristics highlights Oakland's racial and economic disparities and the flaws in the justice system.

How are they a role model?

Towards the end of the book, Richard shows remorse, learns from mistakes, and seeks redemption.

What is their backstory?

Richard grew up in a tough neighborhood. He grew up with family struggles but had a caring mother and mentors.

What is their relationship with the secondary characters?

Richard loves his mother, has a strong bond with friends, and later regrets hurting Sasha.

What is their personality?

Richard is charismatic, impulsive, kind-hearted, but constantly influenced by his environment.
