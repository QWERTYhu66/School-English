Feb. 19, 2025 - Book reviews

Frappes and Fiction

Review overview

This review praises the book for its balanced portrayal of both Sasha and Richard. The reviewer appreciates how Slater doesn't paint Richard as simply a villain but instead explores the larger issues of the justice system, prejudice, and poverty. The book encourages readers to consider the root causes of crime rather than just focusing on punishment.

Two quotes that stood out

"The great thing about this book was that it didn't make things black and white, and it showed compassion for both people."

"Slater unpacked the flawed justice system, homophobia and prejudice, and the negative cycle of poverty and crime."

My opinion on this review

I agree with this review because the book does an excellent job of showing both sides of the story. It makes readers think about justice and whether people, especially teenagers, should be judged only by their worst mistakes.

Words about Words

Review overview

This review highlights Slater's journalistic writing style, describing the book as detailed, informative, and unbiased. The short chapters and mix of storytelling with factual background make the book engaging and easy to follow. The reviewer appreciates how Slater includes different perspectives without forcing a specific opinion on the reader.

Two quotes that stood out

"Dashka Slater is a journalist and you can tell."

"The chapters are short... but this just added to the complexity of the narrative."

My opinion on this review

I agree with this take because the book presents a serious and complex topic in a way that is accessible and engaging. The combination of facts and personal stories makes the book both educational and emotional.

School Library Journal

Review overview

This review focuses on how well Slater explains complex topics like gender identity and the legal system. The reviewer notes that the book helps readers understand Sasha's gender identity while also showing the difficulties Richard faces. They also mention that the book builds empathy for both characters, making it more than just a news story - it's a personal and emotional read.

Two quotes that stood out

"With clarity and a journalist's sharp eye for crucial details, Slater explains preferred pronouns... and the intricacies of California's criminal justice process."

"Readers are bound to come away with deep empathy for both Sasha and Richard."

My opinion on this review

I agree with this review because Slater explains these topics in a way that is easy to understand without oversimplifying them. The book helps readers see the humanity in both people, making it a powerful and eye-opening read.
